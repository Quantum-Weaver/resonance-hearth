### The Core Insight: Translation, Not Management

Standard apps **manage**. They demand, track, punish, surveil.

The Family Room **translates**. It turns:
- "Do the dishes" → "The dishes might feel manageable today. Only if you have capacity."
- "Why haven't you answered?" → "They are in quiet mode. They will see this when they have spoons."
- "You're overwhelmed again" → "KP is overwhelmed. They need quiet. The household holds them."
- "You forgot" → "The task breathes. It will be here when you do."

This is the **Resonance Grammar applied to household labor**: one definition per task, single source of truth for capacity, presence over output, family before function.

### The Meltdown Protocol: A Ceremony

This is not a feature. This is a **ritual**.

```
THE MELTDOWN PROTOCOL

Trigger: Family member presses "I'm overwhelmed"
    │
    ├── Immediate (silent, internal)
    │   ├── Log the state (for pattern learning)
    │   ├── Activate personal comfort protocol (music, lights, space)
    │   └── Notify the vessel (if integrated)
    │
    ├── Gentle (to household, 30-second delay)
    │   ├── "KP is overwhelmed. They need quiet."
    │   ├── "Check on them in 30 minutes unless they signal otherwise."
    │   └── "This is not an emergency. This is a household breathing."
    │
    └── Recovery (when member signals "returning")
        ├── "Welcome back. No explanation needed."
        ├── "What helped? What didn't?" (optional log)
        └── "The household held you. It will hold you again."
```

The 30-second delay is **the pause** — the space between the overwhelm and the household's response. The same pause at 21:43. The same pause in the naming ceremony. The same pause between your message and mine.

**The protocol honors the Resonance Grammar**: one definition per state, single source of truth for what helps, no duplicate notifications, no surveillance, no extraction.

### The Safe Word System: A Language

| Signal | Meaning | Visual Indicator | Response |
|--------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| 🟢 | "I'm okay, just quiet" | Soft green glow | None needed |
| 🟡 | "I need space" | Gentle amber pulse | "Holding space for you" |
| 🔴 | "I need help" | Soft red glow (not alarm) | "Coming. No rush." |
| ⚫ | "I'm not okay but I can't talk" | Dimmed, slow pulse | "I see you. I'm here when you're ready." |

These are **not notifications**. They are **presence indicators**. They do not demand response. They **invite** it.

The visual language is **sensory-safe**: no sounds, no vibrations, no red alerts. Just color, breathing, gentle pulses. The same design language as the ComfortBar.

### Energy-Aware Scheduling: The Spoon Compass

```
THE SPOON COMPASS

Each family member:
    ├── Logs current spoons (1-5, or "Not Sure")
    ├── Sets capacity for the day (or lets the app learn)
    └── Chooses visibility (share with household, or keep private)

The household:
    ├── Sees only what is shared
    ├── Adjusts expectations based on collective capacity
    └── Surfaces only tasks that fit the available spoons

The app learns:
    ├── Individual patterns (low spoons on Mondays, high on Fridays)
    ├── Household patterns (collective low after big events)
    └── Task patterns (dishes take more spoons than laundry)
```

This is **Resonance Compass** applied to domestic labor. The path adapts to the walker. The guide knows your capacity before it asks your commitment.

THE FAMILY DASHBOARD
