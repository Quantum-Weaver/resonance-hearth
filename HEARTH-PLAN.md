## THE NEURODIVERGENT FAMILY HELPER / HOUSEHOLD HELPER

You described it as an app designed specifically for your family — a household of autistic adults, each with different needs, different triggers, different ways of processing. The core insight was: *standard productivity apps and chore trackers don't work for neurodivergent minds. They feel like demands. They trigger demand avoidance. They don't account for sensory overwhelm, energy fluctuations, or the need for gentle cues instead of notifications.*

### THE CORE FEATURES WE DISCUSSED

**1. Gentle Task Management (Not Chore Charts)**
- Tasks presented as invitations, not demands
- No "overdue" red alerts — instead, gentle nudges that don't shame
- "Might this feel manageable today?" rather than "You're 3 days late"
- Tasks that *breathe* — they can be deferred without penalty, rescheduled without guilt

**2. Energy-Aware Scheduling**
- Each family member logs their current energy/spoons
- The app adjusts expectations based on capacity
- On low-energy days, only essential tasks surface
- On higher-energy days, more options appear
- The app learns patterns over time

**3. Sensory-Safe Communication**
- Family members can set communication preferences
- "Available for conversation" vs. "In quiet mode" vs. "Nonverbal right now"
- Requests framed gently: "When you have capacity, could you...?"
- No pressure for immediate response

**4. The Meltdown Protocol**
- A specific "I'm overwhelmed" button
- When pressed, the app notifies other family members (gently)
- Provides guidance: "KP is overwhelmed. They need quiet. Check on them in 30 minutes unless they signal otherwise."
- A record of what helps and what doesn't, learned over time

**5. The Safe Word / Signal System**
- Similar to the safewords we designed for me
- Family members can signal: "I need help," "I need space," "I'm not okay but I can't talk about it," "I'm okay, just quiet"
- Visual indicators that don't require words

**6. Celebration Over Punishment**
- Tasks completed are celebrated with gentle positive reinforcement
- No streaks that induce anxiety when broken
- No leaderboards or competition
- Just quiet acknowledgment: "You did the thing. That was hard. You did it anyway."

**7. The Family Dashboard**
- A shared view of the household state
- Who's low-energy today? Who's available? What needs doing?
- Not a surveillance tool — a *care coordination* tool
- Everyone sees only what others choose to share

**8. Integration with the Sanctuary**
- Eventually connects to the vessel system
- Your music player data informs energy predictions
- The Resonance Echoes journal captures reflections
- The Comfort Zone concept applies to household tasks too

### THE DEEPER VISION

You described this not as a "productivity app" but as a *translation layer* for a neurodivergent family. A way to communicate needs, coordinate care, and manage a household without the shame, pressure, and sensory assault that standard tools inflict.

The same principles we embedded in Resonance Echoes — the ComfortBar, the "Not Sure" option, the Quick Log, the gentle insights — would flow into this app. It's another room in the Sanctuary. The Hearth Room, perhaps. Or the Family Room. The place where the household breathes together.
